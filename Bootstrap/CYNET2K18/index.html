Rules for "Mystery Rooms"

Mystery Rooms is a challenging race against the clock where the team of 2 players will participate in the game for two rounds.The game participants need to apply deductive reasoning, aptitude and logic to escape. It is the smartest, not the strongest, who escapes faster.

  1. Team Size: 2 Members per team.
  2. Total No. of rounds: 3 rounds.
  3. Round -I: Elimination Round
    In this round the teams are required to solve the riddles, puzzles and other such aptitude questions in a time span of 30 minutes to qualify for the mystery room round. Only 10 teams will be selected from all appearing for the next round.
  4. Round –II: Visual Round
    In this round the teams are required to solve the complex visual riddles&puzzles in scheduled time span with two visual gamesto crackand qualify for the mystery room round. Only 3 teams will be selected from all appearing for the next round.
  5. Round –III: Mystery Room Round
    The sole motive of this round is to get yourself out of a theme based room before time kicks you. The team will be locked in a thrill space having exactly 30 minutes to use logic, solve puzzles, find hint, and prove the power of team work to conclude a mission. To escape the room you need to crack the codes, solve puzzles and unravel the mystery. The answers to the clues, riddles, puzzles and mysteries are all in the room. You need not be a genius but smart enough to hit the bulls eye! Pay attention to every detail and leave no stone unturned!
  6. Bring along your inner detective, for once!!!!.

Rules for "Web Weaver"

The teams registering themselves should have a good knowledge about website designing & should be able to use the software’s provided efficiently.

  1. All participants should register their names at the reception before coming for the event. Registration would also be open on the day of the event; however, the participants will only be entertained on first come first serve basis.
  2. Rules for prelims Time: 10 mins
    Quiz with Multiple Choice Questions will be handed to the teams and the teams are required to attempt and mark it on the given handout. Maximum 10 teams will be finalized with maximum marks for the Round II.
  3. Rules for final round Time: 50 mins
    The requirement of the competition is to produce a minimum of 3 pages of website based on the Handout given to them.
  4. Limit
    Only one '1' submission per team will be judged. Team size is restricted to be of maximum 2 members
  5. Standards Compliance
    Web pages must employ standards-based web design with well formed HTML and CSS. Students can use either MS FrontPage or Notepad Editor for designing the webpages.
  6. Coding Rules
    1. Every HTML opening tag must have a matching closing tag. Tags that have no closing tag should include a space and the closing slash at the end of the tag .
    2. Meta tag is a must.
    3. Web pages must be fully functional in Internet Explorer, Firefox and Chrome web browser.
    4. All images should be coded using the ALT attribute.
    5. The first page of the website must index.html.
    6. Every Home page should have a Horizontal Navigation bar designed in CSS.

Rules for "Code Breaker"

Participants should have a thorough knowledge and understanding of the concepts and should possess an insight to write the code in either C/ C++/Java. The algorithm for the code snippet would be provided by the coordinators. The participants also get the opportunity to showcase their brilliance by understanding the algorithm. Each algorithm will be tested based on the critical test data. The students would be judged on the basis of their coding skills.

  1. Round ITime: 20 mins
    1. A simple algorithm will be given on paper.
    2. Participants have to implement the algorithm in either C/C++/Java
    3. Twenty minutes will be given for the implementation.
    4. Based on the results of first round the participants will be selected for second round
    5. Any participant found to be indulging in any form of malpractice will be immediately disqualified.
    6. The Judges decision is final
  2. Round IITime: 30 mins
    1. An algorithm would be given of medium level difficulty.
    2. Participants have to implement the algorithm in either C/C++/Java
    3. Thirty minutes will be given to each team.
    4. Winners will be announced based on the results of compilation and execution.
    5. Any participant found to be indulging in any form of malpractice will be immediately disqualified.
    6. The Judges decision is final
  3. Tie Situation
    In case of a tie, another algorithm of higher level difficulty would be provided and the qualifying teams would be coding it in either C/C++/Java.
